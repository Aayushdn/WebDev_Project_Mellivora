ONE STOP SNOW SHOP

Stories around the world

I’ve been skiing since I was nine and travelled the world chasing great slopes, but nothing tops last summer in the Roaring Fork Valley. Aspen was easy to reach — the airport’s ten minutes away, and free buses link all four mountains: Ajax, Snowmass, Aspen Highlands, and Buttermilk. These slopes were no walk in the park, with steep pitches and long runs that kept me sharp. Hiking Highland Bowl and carving down Ruthie’s Run were unforgettable. Each mountain had its own vibe — Snowmass wide and open, Ajax classic and fast, Buttermilk smooth to finish the week. Evenings at Ajax Tavern or The Red Onion wrapped up perfect days in the Rockies.
- Jake M. (Age: 33)
Aspen, Colorado

I thought I knew snow until I rode in Niseko. The powder is unreal — light, dry, and endless. Every morning brought fresh tracks under Mount Yōtei’s shadow, with easy shuttles between Grand Hirafu, Hanazono, and Annupuri. The tree runs were pure joy, and the locals couldn’t be more welcoming. After a long day, soaking in an onsen while snow fell was unbeatable. Niseko isn’t just a resort — it’s a rhythm of snow, food, and warmth that stays with you.
Hannah T. (Age: 29)
Niseko, Hokkaido, Japan

Chamonix is the Alps at their best — rugged, dramatic, and alive with history. Standing atop the Aiguille du Midi, staring at Mont Blanc, was unforgettable. The Vallée Blanche run tested every skill I had, and the town’s energy is electric. Mornings at Elevation 1904, evenings at La Terrasse — everything revolves around adventure. Chamonix isn’t just a trip; it’s a milestone for anyone who lives for the mountains.
- Liam S. (Age: 36)
Chamonix, Haute-Savoie, France
